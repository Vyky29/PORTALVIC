INTRODUCTION

ClubSENsational Ltd (“ClubSENsational”, “we”, “us”) provides specialist programmes and sessions for participants. We work collaboratively with Local Authorities, NHS bodies, schools, colleges, social care teams, education providers and other organisations that fund or purchase services on behalf of a participant (each a “commissioning organisation”, “you”).

These Terms & Conditions set out how places are reserved, how Purchase Orders and funding confirmation work, how invoicing and payment operate (including payment in arrears where agreed), and how we protect continuity of service for participants. They are separate from the Terms & Conditions that apply to families and private payers.

1. SCOPE AND ACCEPTANCE

These terms apply whenever a commissioning organisation reserves, funds, commissions or purchases ClubSENsational services for a participant.

Acceptance may occur through any of the following:

  • Written confirmation;
  • Approval of a placement;
  • Issue of a Purchase Order;
  • Signing a service agreement;
  • Confirmation by email; or
  • Requesting that a place or agreed timetable is reserved.

Any purchase conditions of the commissioning organisation that contradict these terms will only apply if ClubSENsational has accepted them expressly in writing.

2. REFERRAL, ASSESSMENT AND PLACEMENT APPROVAL

  • A referral or panel approval does not, by itself, guarantee a place.
  • The place, timetable, frequency, duration, venue and type of support must be confirmed by ClubSENsational.
  • Availability may change until the reservation is formally confirmed.
  • We may request relevant information, documentation, risk assessments, support plans and contact details before the start.

3. PURCHASE ORDERS AND FUNDING CONFIRMATION

A valid Purchase Order (PO) or formal written funding authorisation is required.

The PO must correctly reflect:

  • Participant name or agreed reference;
  • Service;
  • Number and frequency of sessions;
  • Session rate;
  • Agreed start date;
  • Funding period;
  • Total approved value; and
  • Correct invoicing details.
  • If the PO is incorrect or incomplete, it must be updated.
  • Delay in the commissioning organisation’s internal process does not change previously agreed dates, rates or commitments.

4. RESERVATION OF PLACES

Once a place, timetable and start date have been agreed, the place is reserved exclusively for the participant and is no longer available to other families or commissioning organisations. Charges therefore commence from the agreed reservation or start date.
  • Once reservation and start date are agreed, the place is blocked exclusively for the participant.
  • That place is no longer available to other families, participants or organisations.
  • Charges begin from the agreed reservation date or start date, even if the participant cannot yet attend because a PO or formal authorisation is outstanding.
  • Invoicing is based on reserved capacity, instructor time, timetable and resources — not solely on attendance.
  • Absence or delay of a Purchase Order does not postpone the start of charges once the place is reserved.
  • If the commissioning organisation does not want charges to begin, it must confirm this in writing before the place is blocked.
  • ClubSENsational may release the place if required documentation is not received within the stated timeframe.

Important: “Reserved and chargeable” is not the same as “approved to attend”. Attendance requires a valid PO or formal written funding authorisation acceptable to ClubSENsational (see Section 5).

5. ATTENDANCE BEFORE RECEIPT OF A PO

  • The participant may not begin attending until ClubSENsational has received a valid Purchase Order or formal written funding authorisation acceptable to ClubSENsational.
  • The place may remain reserved and continue to be chargeable during this period.
  • The commissioning organisation remains responsible for charges from the agreed reservation date.
  • Any exception must be approved expressly by a ClubSENsational director.

6. INVOICING ARRANGEMENTS

  • Invoices may be issued in advance to allow administrative processing.
  • Where the commissioning organisation operates in arrears, ClubSENsational may submit the invoice up to 15 days before the end of the billed period.
  • Submitting an invoice early does not make payment due before the end of the corresponding period (unless a different due date is shown and agreed).
  • Each invoice will show clearly: invoice date; service period; PO number; amount due; and payment due date.
  • Invoices will be sent to the contact or portal specified by the commissioning organisation.
  • The commissioning organisation is responsible for providing correct invoicing details and notifying us of changes.

7. PAYMENT IN ARREARS

Some commissioning organisations operate on payment in arrears. Where agreed in writing, ClubSENsational may accept payment in arrears.

  • Payment in arrears means payment is made after the billed period has ended.
  • It does not mean an invoice may remain unpaid indefinitely after its due date.
  • The payment period runs from the agreed date or the due date shown on the invoice.
  • Internal procedures, approvals, portal delays or temporary absence of a PO do not automatically extend the due date.
  • Any dispute must be notified promptly in writing, stating exactly which part of the invoice is questioned.
  • The undisputed portion must be paid within the stated timescale.

8. LATE PAYMENT AND CREDIT CONTROL

Payment in arrears is an agreed payment method. Late payment means payment received after the invoice due date. There is no charge merely for paying in arrears where that method has been agreed.

Where an invoice remains unpaid after the due date, ClubSENsational may:

  • Send payment reminders;
  • Escalate to the relevant finance or commissioning team;
  • Request immediate confirmation of the payment date;
  • Place the commissioning account under credit review;
  • Refuse new referrals or additional reserved places;
  • Withhold confirmation of future placements;
  • Require written funding assurance before continuing or expanding services;
  • Review whether further services can be provided on credit;
  • Recover reasonable debt recovery or administrative costs where contractually agreed or legally permitted; and
  • Apply contractual or statutory late-payment interest where applicable.

Any late-payment interest rate or recovery fee will be as set out in the applicable schedule or finance settings communicated in writing by ClubSENsational. No fixed late-payment fee applies solely because payment in arrears has been agreed.

9. ACADEMIC-YEAR FUNDING AND FIXED-WEEK CONTRACTS

Where funding is approved for a fixed number of weeks or sessions within an academic year, all charges relating to that academic year must be scheduled for payment within the same funding period. Instalments must not extend into the following academic year unless expressly agreed in writing by ClubSENsational.
  • All charges for a fixed-week or fixed-session academic-year award must be invoiced and paid within that academic year or agreed funding period.
  • A 38-week contract must not automatically be spread across 52 weeks if that pushes payment into the next academic year.
  • Payments may be split into monthly instalments, but the schedule must respect the real service period.
  • If a participant starts in April and the academic-year service ends in July, April–July charges must be settled by July at the latest, unless an exceptional written agreement applies.
  • Payments for one academic year must not continue into September or later months when fees, sessions, frequency, timetable, support, venue, funding approval or PO value need review or renewal.
  • The commissioning organisation must not assume that prior-year rates and conditions remain valid.

10. NEW ACADEMIC YEAR AND PO RENEWAL

  • Before September, or before a new academic year starts, ClubSENsational must receive a new Purchase Order or formal written confirmation that an existing PO remains valid with sufficient value.
  • The PO must be reviewed and updated when fees, session numbers, duration, frequency, service type, venue, or staffing/support level change.
  • ClubSENsational is not obliged to continue a place under outdated rates, quantities or conditions.
  • Continuity depends on availability, funding confirmation and acceptance of updated conditions.
  • Lack of an updated PO may prevent attendance, while charges may continue if the commissioning organisation has asked for the place to remain reserved.

11. FEE REVIEWS AND PRICE CHANGES

  • Fees may be reviewed annually.
  • Fees may also be reviewed when complexity, staffing ratio, duration, venue, operating costs or support level change.
  • ClubSENsational will give reasonable advance notice of changes.
  • A PO must be updated to reflect any new rate.
  • Continuing the service after the effective date of a new rate, once communicated and agreed in writing, constitutes acceptance of the updated rate.

12. CHANGES TO SESSIONS OR FUNDING

  • Changes to days, times, frequency, duration or service must be approved by ClubSENsational.
  • A reduction in funding does not automatically change an existing reservation.
  • The commissioning organisation must give written notice and respect the applicable notice period.
  • Changes take effect only when ClubSENsational confirms them in writing.
  • If a PO is exhausted before the end of the period, it must be increased or a new PO issued.
  • ClubSENsational is not responsible for interruption caused by insufficient authorisation or a PO without available funds.

13. CANCELLATIONS AND NOTICE

These terms use a written notice period appropriate to commissioning organisations (distinct from the family cancel-and-refund administration charge).

  • To end a place, the commissioning organisation must give four (4) calendar weeks’ prior written notice to ClubSENsational, unless a different period is agreed in writing for that placement.
  • Panel approval withdrawal or internal administrative removal of funding does not, by itself, cancel the reservation.
  • ClubSENsational must receive formal written notice.
  • Charges continue during the notice period.
  • Unused sessions during the notice period are not refundable unless agreed in writing.
  • Participant absences do not cancel the payment obligation.

14. PARTICIPANT ABSENCE AND NON-ATTENDANCE

  • Reserved sessions remain chargeable when the participant does not attend.
  • This includes illness, holidays, transport issues, family decisions, school closure or lack of external staff, unless a written exception applies to that placement.
  • If ClubSENsational cancels a session and does not offer a suitable alternative, the existing corporate credit / replacement / adjustment practice for club-cancelled sessions applies (as for family programmes: session credit, or refund of that credit if the place is not continuing).
  • No separate credit system is created by these terms beyond that corporate practice.

15. SUSPENSION AND SAFEGUARDING

ClubSENsational may temporarily suspend attendance where:

  • There is no valid PO or authorisation;
  • Invoices are overdue for a prolonged period;
  • Information needed to deliver the service safely is missing;
  • There are safeguarding, health and safety or staffing risks; or
  • The commissioning organisation does not respond to essential requests.

Any suspension will be managed proportionately and professionally, taking account of the participant’s wellbeing.

16. DISPUTES AND ESCALATION

  1. Query raised with the ClubSENsational finance or administration team.
  2. Review of the invoice, PO and agreed service.
  3. Escalation to a ClubSENsational director where unresolved.
  4. Escalation to the commissioning manager or finance lead.
  5. Written resolution or payment plan where appropriate.

Disputes must not be used to delay payment of undisputed amounts.

17. AUTHORITY TO APPROVE SERVICES

  • The person who confirms a place, requests reservation or authorises the start represents that they have sufficient authority to act for the commissioning organisation.
  • If that person cannot issue the PO, they must provide details of the responsible officer.

18. RECORDS AND COMMUNICATIONS

  • Material confirmations must be in writing.
  • ClubSENsational may retain emails, approvals, POs, invoices, funding confirmations and requested changes.
  • Telephone calls that affect prices, dates, funding or reservations should be confirmed afterwards by email.
  • Commissioning organisation contacts must be kept up to date.

19. DATA PROTECTION AND CONFIDENTIALITY

Personal data about participants and contacts will be handled in accordance with UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and ClubSENsational’s Privacy Policy (published on our website). Data may be shared lawfully for safeguarding, service delivery, commissioning and invoicing. Both parties must treat confidential information appropriately.

These terms do not replace the Privacy Policy; they refer to it.

GLOSSARY

  • Participant — the individual for whom services are commissioned.
  • Commissioning organisation — the entity funding or purchasing services on behalf of a participant.
  • Purchase Order / PO — the organisation’s formal purchase / funding instrument.
  • Reserved and chargeable — the place is blocked and generates charges from the agreed date.
  • Approved to attend — valid PO or formal written funding authorisation is in place so the participant may attend.
  • Payment in arrears — agreed payment after the billed period ends.
  • Late payment — payment after the invoice due date.
